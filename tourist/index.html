Tourist Places

Monas (National Monument)

The National Monument in Jakarta is an iconic landmark symbolizing Indonesia’s independence. Visitors can go up to the observation deck for a panoramic view of the city.

Kota Tua (Old Town)

Kota Tua features Dutch colonial buildings, museums, and lively street performers, making it a cultural hotspot for tourists.

Ancol Dreamland

A popular seaside destination in Jakarta with beach, theme park, water park, and other fun family-friendly attractions.

Istiqlal Mosque and Cathedral Church

Istiqlal is the largest mosque in Southeast Asia, known for its modern architecture and spiritual significance. Meanwhile, the Jakarta Cathedral is a beautiful neo-Gothic church built in the early 1900s. It's directly facing the Istiqlal Mosque, symbolizing religious harmony in Indonesia. There is even an underground tunnel that connect them both.

Ragunan Zoo

A spacious zoo home to over 3,000 animals, including endangered Indonesian species like orangutans and komodo dragons.

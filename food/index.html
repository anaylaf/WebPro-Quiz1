Local Food

Soto Betawi

A beef soup from Jakarta, slow-cooked in a rich and creamy broth made with coconut milk.

Kerak Telor

A traditional Betawi-style omelet made from glutinous rice, duck or chicken egg, and seasoned with fried shallots, dried shrimp, and grated coconut. It’s cooked over charcoal and served crispy on the outside with a savory, slightly spicy flavor.

Kue Rangi

A traditional snack made from tapioca starch, grated coconut, salt, and water. It’s cooked in a special mold and served with a drizzle of melted palm sugar.

Roti Buaya

A sweet bread shaped like a crocodile, often served at weddings as a symbol of loyalty and lasting love. Made from soft, slightly sweet dough and sometimes filled with chocolate or cheese.

Kembang Goyang

A flower-shaped snack made from rice flour, eggs, and coconut milk. Fried using a special mold that’s shaken in hot oil, creating a crispy and lightly sweet treat.

Es Selendang Mayang

A dessert made from rice flour and sago flour, colored in layers of red, white, and green. It’s served chilled with coconut milk, palm sugar syrup, and ice for a sweet and refreshing treat.
